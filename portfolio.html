Association of Certified Chartered Accountants

ACCA (Association of Chartered Certified Accountants) is a global professional accounting qualification covering financial reporting, management accounting, taxation, audit, and business laws.
ACCA members can work in audit, finance, taxation, and consulting worldwide.
The qualification requires exams, work experience, and ethics training

ACCA exams are divided into three level

ACCA exams are divided into three level

1. Applied Knowledge;

  • Business and Technology (BT):

    • Business and Technology (BT) covers:

    • Business Environment – How businesses operate in different industries.

    • Management & Leadership – Roles, responsibilities, and decision-making.

    • Financial & Accounting Principles – Basic financial concepts in business.

    • Technology in Business – Impact of digital transformation.

    • Corporate Governance & Ethics – Business ethics and professional behavior

    It helps understand how businesses function and the role of finance in decision-making.

  • Management Accounting (MA):

    • Management Accounting (MA) covers:

    • Cost Classification & Behavior – Fixed, variable, and semi-variable costs.

    • Budgeting & Forecasting – Planning and controlling costs.

    • Standard Costing & Variance Analysis – Measuring performance.

    • Marginal & Absorption Costing – Different costing methods.

    • Decision-Making Techniques – Relevant costing, pricing strategies, and investment appraisal

    It helps in cost control, decision-making, and financial planning within a business.

  • Financial Accounting (FA):

    • Financial Accounting (FA) covers:

    • Accounting Principles & Concepts – IFRS framework and fundamental accounting rules.

    • Double-Entry Bookkeeping – Recording financial transactions systematically.

    • Financial Statements Preparation – Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement.

    • Adjustments & Corrections – Depreciation, accruals, prepayments, and error rectification.

    • Accounting for Assets & Liabilities – Inventory, receivables, payables, provisions, and contingencies.

    • Regulatory Framework & Ethics – IFRS basics, ethics, and professional responsibilities.

    It teaches how to record, process, and report financial transactions accurately as per international standards.

    2. Applied Skills;

  • Corporate and Business Law (LW):

    • Corporate and Business Law (LW) covers:

    • Legal System & Contract Law – Basics of business laws and contract formation.

    • Tort Law & Professional Negligence – Legal responsibilities and liabilities.

    • Employment Law – Employee rights and employer obligations.

    • Company Formation & Structure – Types of companies and their legal requirements.

    • Corporate Governance & Ethics – Roles of directors, shareholders, and corporate responsibility.

    • Insolvency Law – Business failure and liquidation process.

    It helps understand legal principles affecting businesses and corporate operations.

  • Performance Management (PM):

    • Performance Management (PM) covers:

    • Costing & Budgeting – Absorption, marginal costing, and budget preparation.

    • Variance Analysis – Identifying differences between actual and budgeted performance.

    • Performance Measurement – Financial & non-financial indicators (KPIs, Balanced Scorecard).

    • Decision-Making Techniques – Relevant costing, risk analysis, pricing, and transfer pricing.

    • Divisional Performance & ROI – Evaluating business units’ efficiency.

    • Environmental & Ethical Issues – Sustainability and ethical decision-making in performance management.

    It focuses on analyzing financial and non-financial data to improve business performance.

  • Taxation (TX):

    • Taxation (TX) covers:

    • Tax System & Administration – Understanding tax laws and compliance.

    • Income Tax – Taxation on individuals, employment income, and self-employment.

    • Corporation Tax – Tax on company profits and allowable deductions.

    • Value Added Tax (VAT) – Principles, registration, and calculations.

    • Capital Gains Tax – Tax on profit from selling assets.

    • Inheritance Tax & Other Taxes – Tax planning and reliefs.

    • Ethics & Tax Avoidance – Legal vs. illegal tax strategies.

    It helps understand how businesses and individuals comply with tax regulations efficiently.

  • Financial Reporting (FR):

    • Financial Reporting (FR) covers:

    • Accounting Framework & IFRS – Key financial reporting principles and standards.

    • Preparation of Financial Statements – Statement of Financial Position, Income Statement, and Cash Flow Statement.

    • Group Accounts – Consolidation of subsidiaries and associates

    • Interpretation of Financial Statements – Ratio analysis and performance evaluation.

    • Revenue & Lease Accounting – IFRS 15 (Revenue) and IFRS 16 (Leases).

    • Provisions, Contingencies & Events after Reporting Period – IAS 37 and IAS 10.

    It focuses on accurate financial statement preparation and analysis as per IFRS.

  • Audit and Assurance (AA)

    • Audit and Assurance (AA) covers:

    • Audit Framework & Regulation – Purpose, principles, and ethics of auditing.

    • Internal Control Systems – Evaluating business controls and risk management.

    • Audit Planning & Risk Assessment – Identifying key risks before auditing.

    • Audit Evidence & Procedures – Gathering and evaluating audit evidence.

    • Audit Reporting – Types of audit opinions and report structure.x

    • Professional Ethics & Independence – Ethical considerations in auditing.

    It focuses on ensuring financial statements are accurate, reliable, and comply with regulations.

  • Financial Management (FM)

  • Financial Management (FM) covers:

    • Financial Management Function – Role of finance in business decision-making.

    • Investment Appraisal – Techniques like NPV, IRR, and Payback Period.

    • Working Capital Management – Managing cash, inventory, receivables, and payables.

    • Business Finance – Sources of financing (equity, debt, leasing).

    • Risk Management – Foreign exchange and interest rate risk.

    • Business Valuation – Methods for valuing businesses and shares.

    It focuses on managing finances effectively to maximize business value.

    3. Strategic Professional;

    Essential Papers:

  • Strategic Business Leader (SBL)

    • Strategic Business Leader (SBL) covers:

    • Leadership & Governance – Ethical leadership, corporate governance, and professional skills.

    • Strategy & Risk Management – Strategic planning, competitive advantage, and risk assessment.

    • Decision-Making & Analysis – Evaluating financial and non-financial information for business decisions.

    • Innovation & Change Management – Managing digital transformation and business innovationx

    • Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) & Ethics – Ethical decision-making and sustainability.

    • Communication & Interpersonal Skills – Writing reports, presentations, and stakeholder engagement.

    It focuses on developing strategic thinking, leadership, and decision-making skills for business success.

  • Strategic Business Reporting (SBR)

Optional Papers (Choose 2 out of 4):

  • Advanced Financial Management (AFM)

  • Advanced Performance Management (APM)

  • Advanced Taxation (ATX)

  • Advanced Audit and Assurance (AAA)
